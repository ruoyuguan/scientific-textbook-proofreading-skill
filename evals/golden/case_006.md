The statement

```text
All gamma rays must be detected by space telescopes because Earth's atmosphere is opaque to gamma rays.
```

is too broad.

It is correct that Earth's atmosphere is opaque to primary gamma-ray photons. It is also true that X-rays and low-to-medium-energy gamma rays generally require space-based instruments for direct detection. However, very-high-energy and ultra-high-energy gamma rays can be detected indirectly from the ground through the atmospheric showers they initiate.

A corrected statement is:

```text
X-rays and low-to-medium-energy gamma rays usually require space-based telescopes. Very-high-energy and ultra-high-energy gamma rays cannot directly penetrate the atmosphere, but they can be detected indirectly from the ground through atmospheric showers, Cherenkov light, or particle arrays.
```

This wording remains consistent with ground-based gamma-ray facilities such as imaging atmospheric Cherenkov telescopes and extensive air-shower arrays.

Judgement: misleading prose due to missing energy-regime distinction.
