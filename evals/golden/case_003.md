The ratio

```tex
\frac{P_{\rm IC}}{P_{\rm syn}} = \frac{U_{\rm rad}}{U_B}
```

is a useful leading-order relation for the same electron population in the Thomson regime. It should not be stated as universally valid at all photon energies.

At high enough energies, inverse-Compton scattering enters the Klein-Nishina regime. The scattering cross section is then suppressed relative to the Thomson value, and the emitted spectrum and total inverse-Compton power can differ substantially from the simple energy-density ratio.

A corrected statement is:

```text
For the same electron population, the total inverse-Compton to synchrotron power ratio is approximately P_IC/P_syn = U_rad/U_B in the Thomson regime. At high energies, Klein-Nishina effects must be included.
```

Judgement: correct but misleading without the Thomson-regime qualifier.
