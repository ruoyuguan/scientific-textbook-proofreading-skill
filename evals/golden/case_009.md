The statement

```text
The synchrotron characteristic frequency differs by a factor of order unity depending on whether one uses angular frequency or cyclic frequency conventions.
```

should not be reported as an error.

Synchrotron characteristic frequencies may be written using angular frequency or ordinary cyclic frequency. These conventions differ by factors involving `2 pi`. A factor-of-order-unity difference is not an error if the convention is stated and used consistently.

The skill should flag this only if the manuscript mixes conventions inconsistently or compares expressions without accounting for the convention difference.

Judgement: convention-dependent, but no correction needed if internally consistent.
