The statement

```text
The 2019 EHT image of M87* directly proved general relativity in the strong-field regime.
```

is too strong.

The EHT image provides important evidence for a compact object whose ring-like emission morphology and central brightness depression are consistent with expectations for a Kerr black hole in general relativity. However, the inference depends on interferometric image reconstruction, plasma emission modelling, ray tracing, and comparison with GRMHD models.

A better statement is:

```text
The 2019 EHT image of M87* is consistent with the Kerr black-hole shadow predicted by general relativity and provides an important observational test of strong-gravity black-hole models.
```

Avoid wording such as “directly proved” unless the surrounding text carefully explains the model dependence and the specific tested observables.

Judgement: overstrong claim; correct direction but misleading wording.
